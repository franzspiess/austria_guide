Wachau Valley — Day Trip from Vienna
Dürnstein, Wachau
Day1
Vienna — Day Trip to Dürnstein
🚂 Train Vienna → Krems + bus · ~1.5h each way · Return to Vienna overnight
🚂 Vienna → Dürnstein
The Wachau is one of Austria's most beautiful river valleys — a UNESCO World Heritage Site with vineyard terraces cascading to the Danube. Dürnstein is a tiny baroque village of a few hundred people, with a ruined hilltop castle and legendary evening light. A perfect opening day.
🥾
Hike up to Dürnstein Castle ruins — sweeping views over the Danube bend, vineyards, and apricot orchards. Richard the Lionheart was famously imprisoned here
🏛
The vivid blue Augustinian monastery and the baroque Hauptstraße — tiny but perfectly preserved
🎨
Painting: Danube reflections, vineyard terraces, ruined castle against the sky — classic Wachau compositions
The boat back from Dürnstein to Krems (DDSG Blue Danube) is a beautiful way to end the day if the schedule works.
Salzkammergut Lake District
Hallstatt
Day2
Vienna → Hallstatt · afternoon Gosausee
🚂 Train Vienna → Attnang-Puchheim → Hallstatt Bahnhof · ⛴ Ferry to village · ~3.5h · then 🚌 Bus Gosausee
🚂 Vienna Hbf → Hallstatt🚂 Hallstatt → Gosausee
Arrive in one of Europe's most photographed villages — squeezed between an emerald lake and sheer limestone cliffs. Drop luggage, then take the 30-minute bus to Gosausee for the afternoon. Return to Hallstatt for the evening when the day crowds have gone.
⛴
Ferry arrival from Hallstatt Bahnhof across the lake — the approach to the village by water is unforgettable
🥾
Gosausee (afternoon) — easy 2.5h lake loop with the Dachstein glacier as backdrop. Turquoise water and white glacier walls. One of Austria's finest natural compositions
🚡
Gosausee option: Zwieselalm cable car for elevated glacier views
🎨
Painting: Gosausee turquoise lake + Dachstein glacier; Hallstatt lakeside at dusk without crowds

Bad Ischl & Sankt Wolfgang
Day3
Early Hallstatt → Bad Ischl → Sankt Wolfgang
🚌 Bus Hallstatt → Gosau → Bad Ischl (~1h) · 🚌 Bus → St. Wolfgang (~30min)
🚂 Hallstatt → St. Wolfgang
Morning in Hallstatt before the crowds arrive — the light on the lake before 8am is extraordinary. Then move on via the imperial spa town of Bad Ischl and arrive in Sankt Wolfgang on the Wolfgangsee for the night.
🎨
Early morning Hallstatt — lakeside before 8am, no tourists. The light on the church and coloured houses reflected in calm water is the iconic shot
🏛
Bad Ischl (connection stop, ~45–60min) — Emperor Franz Joseph's summer residence. Kaiservilla, elegant Kurpark, and the famous Zauner Konditorei. A lovely imperial interlude
🏛
Sankt Wolfgang — medieval pilgrimage town on the Wolfgangsee. The Gothic Pacher altarpiece (1481) in the pilgrimage church is one of the finest in Austria
🎨
Painting: Bad Ischl's imperial spa architecture; Wolfgangsee lakefront at Sankt Wolfgang in the evening

Wolfgangsee
St Wolfgang
Overnight in Sankt Wolfgang — much quieter than Hallstatt, beautiful lake views, and perfectly positioned for the Schafbergbahn departure the next morning.
Schafberg & Wolfgangsee
Day4
Schafbergbahn · Wolfgangsee Boat → Salzburg
🚂 Schafbergbahn from St. Wolfgang · ⛴ Boat St. Wolfgang → St. Gilgen · 🚌 Bus → Salzburg
🚂 St. Gilgen → Salzburg
The most varied day of the trip. Ride a historic 19th-century steam cog railway to 1,783m, then descend by lake boat. Already based in St. Wolfgang so there's no morning rush — just walk to the station.
🚂
Schafbergbahn — historic steam cog railway running since 1893, operates May–October. Panoramic summit views over seven Salzkammergut lakes. Featured in The Sound of Music. Book ahead: schafbergbahn.at
🥾
Summit walks at 1,783m — views to the Dachstein and Salzburg Alps. Short routes well-marked from the station
⛴
Wolfgangsee boat — scenic lake cruise St. Wolfgang → St. Gilgen (~45min). Passes lakeside chapels and waterfront villages
🎨
Painting: Summit panorama over the seven lakes; Wolfgangsee from the boat deck at water level
Steam train
Lake boat

Salzburg
Salzburg
Day5
Salzburg — rest day
🏨 Base in the Altstadt · everything walkable
A full day in one of Europe's most beautiful small cities. The UNESCO Altstadt is compact and entirely walkable — baroque architecture, Mozart's birthplace, the fortress above, the Salzach below.
🚡
Festungsbahn funicular to Hohensalzburg Fortress — the panorama over the Altstadt rooftops with the Alps behind is the finest view in the city
🏛
Mirabell Palace & Gardens — formal baroque gardens with Fortress backdrop. Famous Sound of Music location
🏛
Getreidegasse, Residenzplatz, Salzburg Dom — the compact UNESCO Altstadt core, full of baroque detail
🏛
Mozart's Birthplace (Getreidegasse 9) — modest but atmospheric
🎨
Painting: Fortress from Kapitelplatz; the Salzach river and fortress at dusk; Mirabell Gardens geometry
Evening option: Salzburg Festival (July–August) or chamber concerts in Mirabell Palace year-round — book well ahead.

Lake promenade walk around the Zeller See — easy, flat, beautiful reflections of the glacier in the water

Kitzsteinhorn Glacier
Day7
Kitzsteinhorn Glacier · afternoon → Ellmau
🚌 Bus Zell am See → Kaprun (~15min) · 🚡 Glacier cable car · 🚂 Train → Kitzbühel · 🚌 Wilder Kaiser shuttle → Ellmau
🚂 Zell am See → Ellmau
Morning on the glacier — a completely different world from yesterday's lake views. Kitzsteinhorn reaches 3,029m with permanent snow and ice year-round. Then travel east, with a short stroll through Kitzbühel's medieval Altstadt while changing connections, before the shuttle drops you at the foot of the Wilder Kaiser in Ellmau.
🚡
Kitzsteinhorn cable car from Kaprun — multiple stages to 3,029m. The top station sits on the glacier itself with 360° panorama. Snow and ice accessible without equipment in summer
🥾
Marked glacier walks from the top station — short routes on and around the snow fields, dramatic high-alpine environment
🎨
Painting: Glacier ice fields, high-alpine light at 3,000m — a completely different palette: whites, greys, stark blue sky

Kaprun
Kaprun village (8km from Zell) is charming and quiet — worth 30min strolling while waiting for the cable car connection.
Tirol — Wilder Kaiser & Kitzbühel
Ellmau, Wilder Kaiser
Day8
→ Ellmau · Wilder Kaiser full day
🚂 Train Zell am See → Kitzbühel · 🚌 Wilder Kaiser shuttle bus → Ellmau · ~1.5h total
🚂 Zell am See → Ellmau
Ellmau is a small Tyrolean village sitting directly at the foot of the Wilder Kaiser — one of the most dramatic limestone rock faces in the Alps. The jagged 2,344m ridge looms immediately overhead. Overnight here, surrounded by the mountains.
🚡
Hartkaiser gondola from Ellmau — rises directly toward the Wilder Kaiser face to 1,692m. Alpine meadows, marked trails, and the closest elevated views of the rock wall anywhere in the Kaiser area
🥾
Kaisertal valley walk — car-free valley cutting into the Kaiser massif from nearby Going am Wilden Kaiser (10min bus). Extraordinary narrow limestone canyon with a medieval hermit cave (Einsiedelei) at the end
🥾
Going am Wilden Kaiser (neighbouring village, 10min) — one of Austria's most charming tiny Tyrolean villages, directly beneath the rock face. Worth an evening wander
🎨
Painting: The vertical Wilder Kaiser limestone walls from the meadows — unlike anything else in Austria. Tyrolean village with rock face backdrop; gondola meadow views

The Wilder Kaiser shuttle bus connects Ellmau, Going, Scheffau and Söll — easy to explore multiple villages in one day without a car.

East Tyrol — Lienz & the Dolomites
Lienz, East Tyrol
Day10
Lienz — full day
🏨 Based in Lienz · gondolas and old town on foot
Lienz is one of Austria's most underrated towns — a small market town beneath the dramatic Lienzer Dolomiten, where vertical rock faces rise directly above the valley. Genuine, unhurried, and visually extraordinary.
🚡
Zettersfeld gondola — rises into the Lienzer Dolomiten above the town. Alpine meadows, Dolomite rock faces, views down to the Isel-Drau confluence and across to the Glockner group
🚡
Hochstein gondola — alternative lift, similarly dramatic. Both give access to walking trails through Alpine terrain rarely seen by tourists
🏛
Schloss Bruck — medieval castle above the town, houses the Egger-Lienz Museum. Albin Egger-Lienz was from here and painted these landscapes obsessively — fascinating reference for a painter
🏛
Isel and Drau river confluence, Liebburg palace, Lienz Altstadt — compact and walkable with authentic local character
🎨
Painting: Lienzer Dolomiten rock faces looming above town; gondola meadows; Schloss Bruck against mountain backdrop; river confluence below
Dolomiten
Schloss Bruck
Lienz
The Egger-Lienz Gallery inside Schloss Bruck is genuinely worth it for a painter — his landscapes of the East Tyrol are stark and powerful.
Carinthia — Millstatt
Millstatt, Carinthia
Day11
Lienz → Millstatt — full day
🚂 Train Lienz → Spittal/Drau (~45min) · 🚌 Bus → Millstatt (~15min) · Morning departure
🚂 Lienz → Millstatt
Leave Lienz in the morning and arrive in Millstatt with a full day ahead. A gentler, more intimate stop after the drama of the Dolomites — a Romanesque monastery, a long lake promenade, and very few tourists.
🏛
Millstatt Abbey (12th century Romanesque) — one of the finest Romanesque cloisters in Carinthia, peaceful and rarely crowded
🥾
Via Paradiso — the scenic lakeside walking path connecting the villages along the Millstätter See. Flat, beautiful, and serene
⛴
Swimming or boat hire on the Millstätter See — one of Carinthia's cleanest and warmest lakes in summer
🎨
Painting: Millstätter See lakefront reflections; Romanesque monastery cloister; lakeside village scene

Slovenia — Lake Bled
Lake Bled, Slovenia
Day12
→ Lake Bled
🚂 Millstatt → Spittal → Villach · 🚂 Villach → Jesenice · 🚌 Bus → Bled · ~3.5–4h total
🚂 Millstatt → Bled
Cross into Slovenia for the finale — one of the most beautiful lakes in all of Europe. A medieval island church, a clifftop castle, and impossibly turquoise water surrounded by the Julian Alps. The perfect ending to the journey.
⛴
Pletna boat — traditional wooden gondola to Bled Island. Ring the wishing bell in the 17th-century church tower. One of Europe's more magical small experiences
🥾
Bled Castle — clifftop fortress above the lake (15min walk up). Panoramic views over the island, lake, and Julian Alps behind
🥾
Circular lake walk (~6km, 2h flat) — the full perimeter of Lake Bled. One of the finest easy walks in the Alps
🏛
Try a kremšnita (Bled cream cake) at the Park Hotel terrace — a local institution since 1953, not to be skipped
🎨
Painting: Island church from the south shore at dawn; castle reflected in the lake; Julian Alps backdrop; Pletna boat on the water

Bled is also a gateway to further Slovenia: Lake Bohinj (30min bus, wilder and quieter), Vintgar Gorge (4km walk from Bled), or Ljubljana (1.5h by bus) if the journey continues.

Practical Notes

All journeys possible by public transport — ÖBB train, Postbus, and Flixbus.
Book the Schafbergbahn in advance: schafbergbahn.at
Bus 950X Kitzbühel → Lienz: check timetable at postbus.at
Villach → Bled: direct Flixbus or local bus via Jesenice, ~€15–25
ÖBB Sparschiene tickets save significantly if booked in advance: oebb.at

Best months: June and September. July–August is peak season — Hallstatt and Bled are very crowded midday.
